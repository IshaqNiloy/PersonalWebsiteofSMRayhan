Decade 2: The Millennium Era
I left miles away from home for, a business degree, at a reputed university. Being the only son, it has its own challenges but that was worthy.
There I learn the lessons of:
- Stepping out of my comfort zone
- Embracing freedom and responsibility
-Forging meaningful friendships
Two pivotal stories:
1. Rising to the challenge, i turned a friend's skepticism into motivation, graduating among the top 5 students in my class.
2. Additionally, an entrepreneurship course ignited a love for solopreneurship, which would guide my path in next course of life.
After graduation, I Joined Banglalink, a new multinational in telecom industry. I spent a decade in sales and distribution, learning from transformative leaders. Also, I traveled remote corner of my country and found the true essence of mass people. I saw turbulent banglalink rose to market leadership, which is a unique experience for me.
My love for solopreneurship came to meaning when Banglalink offered Voluntary Retirement Scheme. 4 million cheque and believe in self-skills, was enough to leave a secure job.
Then, I got introduced to some similar minded individuals from corporate world. I liked the values and started to build Celero.
Celero was again like Banglalink deja vu. But this time i was calm. With in 1 years, I was the last man beside founder.
Eventually thigns stated to turned around. Celero now have USD 1 billion transactions & over 500 employees.
